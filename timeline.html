September 1, 1939

Germany invades Poland, beginning World War 2.

December 6, 1941

Franklin D. Roosevelt approves The Manhattan Engineering District with
the intentions of creating an atomic bomb.

September 23, 1942

Colonel Leslie Groves (right) is appointed head of The Manhattan Project
on the military side, and Robert Oppenheimer (left) is appointed the Scientific
director.

December 2, 1942

Enrico Fermi at the University of Chicago produces the first controlled
nuclear fission reaction.

April 12, 1945

Franklin Roosevelt passes away and Harry Truman takes his place as the
33rd President of the United States of America.

April 27, 1945

Four cities are selected as possible targets for the atomic bomb:
Kyoto, Hiroshima, Kokura, and Niigata.

May 8, 1945

Germany's unconditional surrender marks the end of war in Europe.
(AKA: Victory in Europe Day, V-E Day, VE Day, V Day).

July 16, 1945

The first atomic detonation takes place at Alamogordo, New Mexico. This test
is known as the Trinity Test.

July 28, 1945

Japan rejects the Potsdam Declaration that called for unconditional surrender.

August 6, 1945

Little Boy is detonated over Hiroshima, Japan.

August 9, 1945

Fat Man is detonated over Nagasaki after deciding the weather was
too poor for a detonation over Kokura.

September 2, 1945

Japan declares formal surrender.
